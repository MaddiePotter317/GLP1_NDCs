GLP1 DETAILS

The generic GLP1 names were sourced from NIPH ATC GLP1 class. To find these, open the NIPH ATC website (https://atcddd.fhi.no/atc_ddd_index/). On the home page there is a Search Query box, type in GLP-1 as shown below. This will take you to the GLP-1 class.


 An important note is that this NDC list also includes Tirzepatide, which is not in the GLP1 generic class, it is in class A10BX Other blood glucose lowering drugs, excl. Insulins, but Tirzepatide is being considered a GLP1 https://www.mayoclinic.org/drugs-supplements/tirzepatide-subcutaneous-route/description/drg-20534045. Here is a list of both brand and generic names: https://www.drugs.com/drug-class/glp-1-receptor-agonists.html and that list is corroborated here: https://my.clevelandclinic.org/health/treatments/13901-glp-1-agonists.  


GLP-1 agonist medications currently available on the U.S. market include: 

Dulaglutide (Trulicity®). 

Exenatide (Byetta®). 

Exenatide extended-release (Bydureon®). 

Liraglutide (Victoza®). 

Lixisenatide (Adlyxin®). 

Semaglutide injection (Ozempic®). 

Semaglutide tablets (Rybelsus®). 

There’s also a similar class of medications called dual GLP-1/GIP receptor agonists. There’s currently one of these medications on the market. It’s called tirzepatide (Mounjaro® or Zepbound).
